## Starting Moves
###### Potion Master
When you begin to **brew a tincture or potion**, describe what effects you want it to have when drunk/splashed/boiled/applied. The GM will tell you zero to 5 of the following:
- you must reference a certain alchemical text to learn the process
- you must get some expensive extra ingredient, the GM will tell you which
- you must brew it under the supervision of a (possibly specific) master
- you must create it with incredibly high heat, pressure, cold, vacuum, or some other limiting condition
- it will take hours/days/weeks
- you have to create it near a Primal Manifestation of one of the four basic elements
- the best you can think of right now is a lesser or somewhat different effect
When you **finish brewing such a potion**, write down it's recipe in your Brews. Ask the GM what you learned which makes brewing it again easier.
###### Brew Lore
You have a list of Brews you can make. When you **spend an hour or so brewing**, make as many simple brews as you have reagents for. In addition to whatever **Potion Master** gives, you can always make Health Potions and 3 more of the following of your choice:
- Health Potion: Boil some fruit juice and slowly stir in mercury. Heals 1d6
- Hydrochloric Acid: Mix sulfuric acid and salt to create a vial of Hydrogen Chloride gas. When it comes into contact with water, the odorless gas produces the acid.
- Firebreath: Melt vitriol and pour it into oil. When you drink it and spit it out, the oil erupts in flames around boiling sulfur.
- Vial of Night: Dissolve fine lead into crushed hyacinth, and pour it into a sealed glass container. When opened, the lead clings to whatever is nearby, and glows if there is magic, or causes supernatural shadow if not.
- Nitrous Oxide: Heat iron filings mixed with nitric acid and 1 Mercury. The gas causes euphoria, and inability to feel pain for a few hours. If carefully applied or 3 potions are dispersed into air, then it instead causes rapid sleep.
- Time Draught: Mix the sap of ancient trees with sal alembroth. When poured on something, time passes incredibly quickly. Skin withers and dies unless cleaned, iron rusts, clay sets.
- Lifebrew: Take Mercury and culture yeast in it. When you pour this on an organism, it grows incredibly quickly: good for plants, bad for animals.
###### Rare Suppliers
You must use reagents to work your alchemy. They weigh 1 load, and they are not difficult to find: when you're in an appropriate area and spend time searching for materials, you can find at least 3. But they might be hard to get or cost coin, or drive some other bargain.
###### Information War
When you meet peacefully with another alchemist, tell them secrets you've learned, and optionally ask them a question. Then they'll tell you something they've learned, related to your question if they know anything.
## Advanced Moves
###### Tinkerer
When you try to alter or inspect a magical device, roll+INT. On a 7+, you know how it works. Additionally, choose 2/1:
- Changing it won't damage it too much
- Changing it won't take that long
- Changing it won't require any special tools
- Changing it won't be very conspicuous
###### Salvager
When you **break down something alchemically magical**, gain a use of Reagents.
